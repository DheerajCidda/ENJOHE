Engineering/Technology Jobs
in Healthcare


Engineering/technology professionals are among the most highly sought-after professionals in the healthcare industry, with increased demand and talent shortages leading to hundreds of thousands of high-salary job opportunities all the time. Candidates with almost all engineering/technology specialties (Biomedical, Chemical, Civil, Computer, Electrical, Environmental, Industrial, Information, Materials, Mechanical, Software, Systems, etc.) may find tremendous career opportunities with massive potential in healthcare. The table below exhibits an overall view of engineering/technology jobs in healthcare, and provides a quick and clear guidance for job seekers to better understand the nature of all engineering/technology jobs in healthcare, thus facilitating a precise and efficient target job search.

Manufacturers and distributors of medical instruments (e.g., blood pressure monitor), medical devices (e.g., surgical robot), imaging machines (e.g., X-ray, MRI), artificial organs, and other non-disposable medical devices as well as medical software.

This is an area heavily relying on engineering/technology  expertise, and where Healthcare Engineering/Technology professionals make major contributions in basic research that leads to technology breakthroughs, as well as subsequent design, development, and manufacturing of medical devices and equipment.

Medical supplies

Manufacturers and distributors of medical supplies used by healthcare providers and the general public, including makers of implants, contact lenses, eyeglass lenses, bandages and other disposable medical supplies.

Healthcare Engineering/Technology professionals contribute to the research & development of new products and manufacturing in terms of process design, machinery, automation, quality control, and cost reduction.  

Healthcare systems

Companies that specialize in healthcare systems including facilities, information, financial, and other systems.

Healthcare Engineering/Technology professionals work with healthcare providers to design, construct, improve, and/or operate healthcare facilities, information, financial, and other healthcare systems, following special regulations.

Pharmaceuticals

Manufacturers of prescription or over-the-counter drugs, such as aspirin, cold remedies and birth control pills, including vaccine producers.

Healthcare Engineering/Technology professionals contribute to the pharmaceutical manufacturing processes in terms of process design, unit operation, quality control, and cost reduction.  

Healthcare consulting, support, and education

Companies that provide consulting service and support to healthcare, as well as continued education to healthcare personnel.

Healthcare Engineering/Technology professionals provide consulting service, support, and education on all aspects of healthcare involving engineering, such as optimizing healthcare operations, solving problems, providing informatics service, as well as designing, planning and/or executing new projects.
